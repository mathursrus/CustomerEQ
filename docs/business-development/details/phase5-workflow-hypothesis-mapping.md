# Phase 5: Workflow Hypothesis Mapping - CX-Loyalty Platform

*Date: March 24, 2026*

## Workflow Research Summary

This phase researches known workflows for the ICP and generates hypothesized Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) patterns to guide customer discovery. Based on extensive research, three distinct workflow patterns emerge with clear pain points and validation opportunities.

---

## Workflow Pattern 1: "The Reactive Response Cycle" (Most Common)

### Current State Workflow

**Monthly/Quarterly Process:**
1. **Survey Deployment** (Week 1)
   - Marketing team deploys NPS/CSAT surveys via Qualtrics/SurveyMonkey
   - 10-15% response rate typical
   - Data collected in CX platform

2. **Data Analysis** (Week 2-3)
   - CX team analyzes feedback, creates reports
   - Identifies trends, segments by customer type
   - Manual export to Excel/PowerPoint for stakeholder review

3. **Action Planning** (Week 4)
   - Cross-functional meeting to discuss findings
   - Marketing team plans retention campaigns
   - Loyalty team adjusts program mechanics separately

4. **Campaign Execution** (Week 5-8)
   - Email campaigns to at-risk customers
   - Loyalty offers deployed through separate platform
   - No connection between specific feedback and specific actions

5. **Results Measurement** (Week 9-12)
   - Separate measurement of CX metrics and loyalty engagement
   - No unified view of feedback-to-action effectiveness

### Pain Points & Friction

**Time Delays:**
- 4-8 weeks from feedback collection to action
- Customer issues may escalate during delay period
- Competitive window for retention closes

**Data Silos:**
- Customer profiles exist separately in CX and loyalty systems
- No unified view of customer sentiment + engagement history
- Manual correlation required for insights

**Manual Processes:**
- Export/import data between systems monthly
- Manual segmentation and campaign setup
- Separate reporting and ROI measurement

**Missed Opportunities:**
- Positive feedback doesn't trigger advocacy programs
- Negative feedback doesn't trigger immediate retention actions
- No real-time response capability

### Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Hypotheses

**Primary JTBD**: "When I receive customer feedback, I want to immediately trigger appropriate loyalty actions so that I can prevent churn and maximize advocacy before the moment passes."

**Supporting JTBDs**:
- "When a customer gives negative feedback, I want to automatically offer a retention incentive so that I can recover the relationship before they churn."
- "When a customer gives positive feedback, I want to invite them to our referral program so that I can turn satisfaction into advocacy."
- "When I analyze feedback trends, I want to see loyalty engagement data in the same view so that I can understand the complete customer story."

## Workflow Pattern 2: "The Integration Struggle" (Mid-Market)

### Current State Workflow

**Technology Stack:**
- **CX Platform**: GetFeedback, Alchemer, or SurveySparrow ($5K-$15K annually)
- **Loyalty Platform**: Yotpo, LoyaltyLion, or Smile.io ($3K-$12K annually)
- **Integration**: Basic API connections or manual processes

**Daily Operations:**
1. **Morning Dashboard Review** (30 minutes)
   - CX manager checks feedback dashboard
   - Loyalty manager checks program performance
   - No unified metrics or alerts

2. **Weekly Data Sync** (2-4 hours)
   - Export customer data from CX platform
   - Import/match with loyalty platform data
   - Manual reconciliation of customer profiles

3. **Monthly Strategy Alignment** (2-hour meeting)
   - CX and loyalty teams share insights
   - Attempt to correlate feedback with engagement
   - Plan coordinated campaigns manually

4. **Quarterly Program Optimization**
   - Separate analysis of CX and loyalty ROI
   - Guesswork on which changes drove improvements
   - Limited ability to test integrated approaches

### Pain Points & Friction

**Resource Drain:**
- 6-10 hours weekly on manual data management
- Dedicated staff time for integration maintenance
- Opportunity cost of manual work vs. strategic initiatives

**Decision-Making Delays:**
- Incomplete data leads to delayed decisions
- Risk aversion due to unclear cause-and-effect
- Quarterly optimization cycles too slow for market changes

**Team Misalignment:**
- CX and loyalty teams have different KPIs
- Conflicting priorities and budget allocation
- Lack of shared success metrics

### Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Hypotheses

**Primary JTBD**: "When I need to understand customer health, I want to see feedback sentiment and loyalty engagement in one unified view so that I can make informed decisions quickly."

**Supporting JTBDs**:
- "When I plan marketing campaigns, I want to segment customers by both satisfaction and loyalty behavior so that I can target the right message to the right audience."
- "When I measure program ROI, I want to see how CX improvements impact loyalty engagement so that I can prove the value of integrated strategies."
- "When I optimize our programs, I want to test changes across both feedback and loyalty simultaneously so that I can understand true impact."

## Workflow Pattern 3: "The Enterprise Orchestration Challenge" (Large Companies)

### Current State Workflow

**Complex Technology Stack:**
- **CX Platform**: Qualtrics or Medallia ($50K-$200K annually)
- **Loyalty Platform**: AnnexCloud or Oracle ($30K-$150K annually)
- **Integration Layer**: Custom APIs, middleware, data warehouse

**Organizational Structure:**
- **CX Team**: 3-8 people, reports to CMO or Chief Customer Officer
- **Loyalty Team**: 2-5 people, reports to Marketing or Retention
- **IT/Data Team**: Manages integrations and data flows

**Monthly Process:**
1. **Data Pipeline Management** (Ongoing)
   - IT team maintains custom integrations
   - Data warehouse aggregates customer touchpoints
   - Regular troubleshooting of sync issues

2. **Advanced Analytics** (Weekly)
   - Data science team creates predictive models
   - Separate models for churn prediction and loyalty optimization
   - Limited cross-platform insights

3. **Strategic Planning** (Monthly)
   - Executive dashboard with separate CX and loyalty metrics
   - Cross-functional meetings to align strategies
   - Complex approval processes for integrated campaigns

4. **Campaign Execution** (Ongoing)
   - Sophisticated segmentation and personalization
   - A/B testing within each platform separately
   - Limited ability to test integrated experiences

### Pain Points & Friction

**Technical Complexity:**
- $100K-$500K annual integration maintenance costs
- 3-6 month implementation timelines for changes
- Risk of system failures affecting customer experience

**Organizational Silos:**
- Different teams optimizing for different metrics
- Complex approval processes slow decision-making
- Difficulty attributing success to integrated efforts

**Scalability Challenges:**
- Custom integrations don't scale with business growth
- Adding new touchpoints requires significant development
- Vendor lock-in limits flexibility

### Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Hypotheses

**Primary JTBD**: "When I need to orchestrate customer experiences at scale, I want a unified platform that eliminates custom integration complexity so that I can focus on strategy instead of technical maintenance."

**Supporting JTBDs**:
- "When I evaluate program performance, I want to see unified attribution across all customer touchpoints so that I can optimize the complete customer journey."
- "When I need to implement new customer experience initiatives, I want to deploy them quickly without months of integration work so that I can respond to market changes rapidly."
- "When I report to executives, I want unified metrics that show the business impact of integrated CX-loyalty strategies so that I can secure continued investment."

## Cross-Pattern Pain Point Analysis

### Universal Pain Points (All Patterns)

1. **Time-to-Action Gap**: 3-8 weeks from feedback to loyalty response
2. **Data Fragmentation**: Customer profiles exist separately in each system
3. **Manual Correlation**: Significant effort required to connect insights
4. **ROI Attribution**: Difficulty measuring integrated program effectiveness
5. **Missed Real-Time Opportunities**: No immediate response to feedback

### Segment-Specific Pain Points

**Small/Mid-Market Specific:**
- Resource constraints limit dedicated integration staff
- Budget pressure to choose between CX or loyalty investment
- Limited technical expertise for complex integrations

**Enterprise Specific:**
- High integration maintenance costs
- Organizational complexity slows decision-making
- Vendor management overhead across multiple platforms

## Validation Questions Framework

### For Workflow Pattern 1 (Reactive Response)
- How long does it currently take from receiving negative feedback to deploying a retention offer?
- What percentage of feedback leads to specific loyalty actions?
- How do you currently measure the effectiveness of feedback-driven campaigns?

### For Workflow Pattern 2 (Integration Struggle)
- How many hours per week does your team spend on manual data management between CX and loyalty platforms?
- What decisions do you delay due to incomplete customer data?
- How do you currently prove ROI on integrated CX-loyalty initiatives?

### For Workflow Pattern 3 (Enterprise Orchestration)
- What does it cost annually to maintain integrations between your CX and loyalty platforms?
- How long does it take to implement new integrated customer experience initiatives?
- What metrics do you wish you had to better understand customer journey effectiveness?

## Hypothesized Solution Workflows

### Target State: "Real-Time Feedback-to-Action Engine"

**Automated Workflow:**
1. **Feedback Capture** (Real-time)
   - Customer submits feedback through any channel
   - AI sentiment analysis and categorization
   - Unified customer profile updated instantly

2. **Intelligent Routing** (< 5 minutes)
   - Negative feedback triggers retention workflow
   - Positive feedback triggers advocacy workflow
   - Neutral feedback triggers engagement optimization

3. **Automated Action** (< 15 minutes)
   - Personalized loyalty offers deployed automatically
   - Customer service alerts for high-priority issues
   - Referral program invitations for advocates

4. **Continuous Learning** (Ongoing)
   - AI optimization based on action effectiveness
   - Predictive modeling for proactive interventions
   - Unified analytics and attribution

### Value Proposition Validation

**Time Savings**: Reduce feedback-to-action time from weeks to minutes
**Resource Efficiency**: Eliminate manual data management overhead
**Revenue Impact**: Increase retention through immediate response
**Strategic Focus**: Shift from operational tasks to strategic optimization
